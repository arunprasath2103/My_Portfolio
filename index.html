Who I am

The person behind
the code

I got into programming because I wanted to build things that actually help people — not just impressive demos. That stubbornness led me to build Gazelink, an eye-tracking communication tool for people with ALS and locked-in syndrome. No special hardware, no calibration rig — just Python, a regular webcam, and a problem worth solving. It works, and that's what matters to me.

I'm a final-year CSE student at Nandha College of Technology in Tiruppur, maintaining a CGPA of 8.17. My technical home is the MERN stack, though I've been spending more time with Python lately — building data pipelines by day, poking at ML problems at night. I solve DSA on LeetCode not because interviews demand it, but because I genuinely enjoy the moment a problem that seemed impossible twenty minutes ago suddenly clicks.

When I'm not coding, I'm deep in Tamil film soundtracks (A.R. Rahman rewired my brain early), reading about brutalist architecture, or wondering why some coffee just tastes like disappointment. I believe the best software is built at the intersection of engineering precision and human empathy — and I'm still figuring out which side pulls harder.
